Random Facts

  • The Eiffel Tower can be 15 cm taller during the summer due to the expansion of the iron in the heat.
  • Bananas are berries, but strawberries aren't. In botanical terms, berries are defined as fleshy fruits produced from a single ovary, and bananas fit this criteria.
  • Honey never spoils. Archaeologists have found pots of honey in ancient Egyptian tombs that are over 3,000 years old and still perfectly edible.
  • The longest recorded flight of a chicken is 13 seconds. This amusing fact highlights the limited flying abilities of domestic chickens.
  • The world's largest desert is not the Sahara, but Antarctica. A desert is defined by its low precipitation, and Antarctica fits this criteria despite being covered in ice.
  • Octopuses have three hearts. Two pump blood to the gills, while the third pumps it to the rest of the body.
  • The shortest war in history was between Britain and Zanzibar in 1896, lasting only 38 minutes.
  • A group of flamingos is called a "flamboyance."
  • The original name for Bank of America was "Bank of Italy." It was founded in San Francisco to serve immigrants who faced discrimination from established banks.
  • Cows have best friends and can become stressed when they are separated from them.
  • The Great Wall of China is not visible from the moon with the naked eye, contrary to popular belief. Astronauts have noted that it's difficult to see without aid.
  • The average person will spend about six months of their life waiting for red lights to turn green.
  • Honeybees can recognize human faces and remember them for their entire lives.
  • The oldest known recipe in the world is for beer. It dates back to around 3,900 BCE and was discovered in a Sumerian poem that detailed the process of brewing.
  • The world's largest known living organism is a fungus in Oregon's Malheur National Forest. It covers an area of over 2,385 acres.
  • The average person will walk around 70,000 miles in their lifetime, which is roughly equivalent to walking around the Earth three times at the equator.
  • A jiffy is an actual unit of time. It is defined as the time it takes for light to travel one centimeter in a vacuum, approximately 33.3564 picoseconds.
